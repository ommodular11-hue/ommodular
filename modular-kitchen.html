01 Configuration

Linear Kitchen

A straight kitchen, also known as a one-wall kitchen, is designed entirely along a single wall. Found in studio apartments and condominiums, it's the ultimate space saver — minimalistic in theme, with a large number of cabinets making a narrow space look wide.

02 Configuration

Parallel Kitchen

A complete space-saver — countertops and shelves run in two parallel lines. One of the oldest and most renowned kitchen styles, double cabinets offer twice the storage and ample workspace for the modern cook.

03 Configuration

L-Shape Kitchen

Two adjoining, perpendicular walls of cabinets forming a formidable 'L'. One of the most versatile and flexible configurations for cooking areas, it opens up the centre of the space for seamless movement.

04 Configuration

U-Shape Kitchen

A three-wall design with one side ideal for access. Most efficient for medium and spacious kitchens — the kitchen work triangle fits perfectly here, highlighting the cabinets and maximising counter space.

05 Configuration

Island Kitchen

A freestanding combination of countertop and cabinet installed at the kitchen's centre — the island becomes the focal point, serving multiple purposes. Utilitarian and aesthetically pleasing in equal measure.

06 Configuration

Peninsula Kitchen

Attached to a wall or adjacent countertop with three accessible sides — it offers workspace and storage of an island without requiring a fully detached structure. The best of both worlds in a connected form.
